# Pixieset Competitive Recon — March 18, 2026

**Source:** Live walkthrough of Kelly's Pixieset account (Meadow Lane Media, Plus plan)

---

## 1. PRICING STRUCTURE

Pixieset charges **per storage tier, monthly subscription** to the photographer. No per-gallery or per-client fees.

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (20% off) | Photo Storage | Video Storage | Key Feature |
|------|---------|-------------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Free** | $0 | — | 3 GB | — | Basic galleries |
| **Basic** | $10 | $96/yr ($8/mo) | 10 GB | 30 min HD | Custom branding, custom domain, unlimited mobile apps, commission-free sales |
| **Plus** | $20 | $192/yr ($16/mo) | 100 GB | 1 hr HD | All Basic features |
| **Pro** | $30 | $288/yr ($24/mo) | 1,000 GB | 2 hr 4K | RAW uploads |
| **Ultimate** | $50 | $480/yr ($40/mo) | Unlimited | 5 hr 4K | Everything |

**Also available:** "The Pixieset Suite" bundle — all 5 products (Client Gallery + Website + Store + Studio Manager + Mobile Gallery App) from $28/mo with up to 37% off.

### Key Pricing Observations:
- **Storage-gated** — the main differentiator between tiers is GB of photo storage
- Free tier exists as funnel (3 GB = ~1-2 small galleries)
- No per-gallery charges, no per-client charges, no download limits visible
- Commission-free print sales on all paid plans (free plan likely charges commission)
- Video storage is a secondary tier differentiator
- Kelly's account: Plus @ $20/mo, using 69.11 GB of 100 GB

### PhotoVault Comparison:
- **PV charges the CLIENT, not the photographer** — fundamentally different model
- PV: $59 (6mo), $100 (1yr), $149 (2yr) per gallery, paid by client
- PV photographer sub: $22/mo (free during beta)
- PV's model is inverted: photographer pays less, client pays for preservation
- **This is our key differentiator** — Pixieset is a photographer cost center, PhotoVault is a revenue generator

## 2. PROOFING WORKFLOW — "Favorites"

Pixieset's proofing is built-in as the "Favorite" system per collection:

### Features:
- **Favorite Photos**: Toggle on/off per collection — clients can heart/favorite photos
- **Favorite Notes**: Toggle on/off — clients can add text notes to favorited photos
- **Preset Favorite Lists**: Photographer creates named lists with selection limits
  - Use cases: album selections, free downloads, retouching picks
  - Sets a max number of selections per list
- **Favorite Activity**: Feed showing when clients create favorite lists (visible in Activities tab and notification panel)

### How it works:
1. Photographer enables Favorites on a collection
2. Optionally creates preset lists with limits (e.g., "Album Picks — max 50")
3. Client browses gallery, clicks heart icon on photos they want
4. Client can add notes to explain choices
5. Photographer sees favorite activity in real-time
6. Photographer reviews selections in Favorite Activity

### Assessment:
- **Built-in, not bolted on** — it's a first-class feature in every collection's settings
- Simple and clean — clients just "heart" photos
- Preset lists with limits is a nice workflow for album proofing
- No approval/rejection workflow visible (it's one-directional: client picks, photographer reviews)

### PhotoVault Opportunity:
- PV doesn't have proofing yet — this is a gap
- But PV's "hero photo" concept could evolve into something better
- Consider: client picks favorites → photographer delivers → hero photos emerge from those
- PV could differentiate with a two-way proofing workflow (client picks, photographer responds)

## 3. GALLERY EXPIRATION

### What I Found:
- **Expiry Date filter** exists on the collections list page (alongside Status, Category Tag, Event Date, Starred)
- **No expiration setting visible** in the per-collection Privacy or General settings
- Expiry appears to be set via the collection **status dropdown** (Published/Draft/Expired)
- Collections stay up as long as the photographer pays their subscription

### Key Insight:
- **Galleries are tied to the photographer's subscription** — if they cancel/downgrade, galleries are at risk
- There's no built-in "this gallery expires in 6 months" model — it's photographer-managed
- Pixieset collections stay live as long as storage quota isn't exceeded
- If photographer cancels → galleries go offline → photos potentially lost

### PhotoVault Advantage:
- **PV galleries are client-paid and independently preserved** — this is a MAJOR differentiator
- Client pays for 6mo/1yr/2yr of hosting regardless of photographer subscription status
- "Your memories don't depend on your photographer's software subscription" — powerful messaging
- Auto-renewal extends preservation indefinitely

## 5. DOWNLOAD LIMITS

### Per-Collection Download Settings:
- **Photo Download**: ON/OFF toggle
- **Photo Download Sizes**:
  - High Resolution: Original or 3600px
  - Web Size: 2048px, 1024px, or 640px
  - Photographer chooses which sizes to offer
- **Video Download**: ON/OFF toggle
- **Download PIN**: Optional 4-digit PIN required to download
- **Advanced Settings tab**: Likely has per-set download controls

### Key Finding:
- **No visible download COUNT limits** — once download is enabled, clients appear to have unlimited downloads
- Control is via size/resolution, not quantity
- The photographer controls what sizes are available, not how many times a client downloads
- Download Activity tracking shows who downloaded what and when

### PhotoVault Comparison:
- PV currently offers downloads as part of the gallery
- PV could differentiate by offering tiered download resolution (web vs. full-res) as part of pricing tiers

## HOW PHOTOVAULT CAN COMPETE AND WIN

### Where Pixieset is Strong (Don't Fight Here):
1. **Breadth of platform** — 5 products, deeply integrated
2. **Design customization** — 8 cover layouts, color themes, grid options
3. **Print store** — automatic fulfillment with labs
4. **Studio Manager** — full CRM/invoicing/contracts
5. **Market dominance** — established brand, massive user base

### Where Pixieset is Vulnerable (Attack Here):

#### 1. **The Pricing Model Inversion** (Our #1 Weapon)
- Pixieset: Photographer pays $10-50/mo forever. Client pays nothing.
- PhotoVault: Client pays $59-149 per gallery. Photographer earns 50% commission.
- **Every gallery is revenue, not cost.** Photographer makes money delivering photos.
- Message: "Stop paying to deliver your work. Start earning from it."

#### 2. **Gallery Permanence** (Our #2 Weapon)
- Pixieset: Gallery dies if photographer cancels subscription or runs out of storage
- PhotoVault: Gallery lives independently, paid for by the client
- **Client's memories aren't hostage to a photographer's subscription**
- Message: "Your photos survive forever — no matter what happens to your photographer's account."

#### 3. **The Family Memory Angle** (Our #3 Weapon)
- Pixieset is delivery-focused: upload → deliver → done
- PhotoVault is preservation-focused: upload → deliver → family treasures it for years
- Hero photo emails, memory reminders, multi-generational sharing
- Pixieset has no emotional post-delivery relationship with the client
- Message: "Pixieset delivers photos. PhotoVault preserves memories."

#### 4. **Revenue Per Client** (Business Case for Photographers)
- Pixieset: Photographer pays $20/mo ($240/yr) regardless of clients
- PhotoVault: Each gallery generates revenue. 20 galleries/year × $100 × 50% = $1,000/yr to photographer
- **Net position**: Pixieset costs $240/yr. PhotoVault earns $1,000/yr.
- Message: "Switch from a $240 annual expense to $1,000+ annual revenue."

#### 5. **Simplicity** (Judo Move)
- Pixieset is complex: 5 products, dozens of settings per collection
- Photographers who just want to deliver photos are overwhelmed
- PhotoVault: Upload → Share → Get Paid. Three steps.
- Message: "Gallery delivery without the MBA."

### What We Need to Build to Compete:

#### Must-Have (Before targeting Pixieset users):
- [ ] **Gallery design options** — at least 3-4 cover layouts, color themes
- [ ] **Proofing/favorites** — client can select favorites, photographer reviews
- [ ] **Download size controls** — offer web vs. full-res options
- [ ] **Password protection** — per-gallery passwords
- [ ] **Custom branding** — photographer logo on gallery

#### Nice-to-Have (Phase 2-3):
- [ ] Gallery sets/albums within a collection
- [ ] Custom domain support
- [ ] Download activity tracking
- [ ] Mobile-optimized gallery app or PWA
- [ ] Print store partnership (WHCC, etc.)

#### Don't Build (Not Our Fight):
- Website builder (Squarespace/Wix own this)
- Full CRM/Studio Manager (HoneyBook/Dubsado own this)
- Contract/invoice system (same)

## SUMMARY

Pixieset is a mature, feature-rich platform charging photographers $10-50/mo for delivery infrastructure. Their weakness is that **galleries are a cost center tied to the photographer's subscription**.

PhotoVault's winning strategy: **Invert the model.** Make galleries a revenue source, make preservation the value prop, and keep it simple. We don't need to match Pixieset feature-for-feature — we need to make the economic argument irresistible and the emotional argument unforgettable.

**The pitch:** "Why pay $240/year to deliver photos when you could earn $1,000/year doing the same thing — and your clients' memories are preserved forever?"
