## Top Intervention Patterns

### 1. "Should I commit/push?" — THE #1 QUESTION (~30% of all direction choices)

Examples from nearly every session:
- "Should I commit and push this fix now?"
- "Should I commit and push?"
- "Want to commit these changes?"
- "Should I commit?"
- "Want me to push?"

**Root Cause:** CLAUDE.md says "NEVER commit unless user says commit" — Claude follows the rule but asks constantly. This is BY DESIGN per your git discipline rules, but it creates the most intervention overhead.

**Recommendation:** This is working as intended. The alternative (auto-committing) is worse per your stated preference. However, you could reduce noise by adopting a convention like "commit and continue" at natural milestones — Claude would stop asking and just queue commits until you say "push."

---

### 2. "What do you want to tackle?" — Session Start Drift (~15% of questions)

Examples:
- "What would you like to tackle?"
- "What would you like to work on?"
- "What do you want to hit?"
- "What do you want to do with all this?"

**Root Cause:** Sessions start without a clear task directive. CURRENT_STATE.md has a "Next action" field but Claude still asks.

**Recommendation:** When starting a session, lead with the task: "Do X" not "Let's work on the project." This eliminates 2-3 questions per session.

---

### 3. Spec Ambiguity — Business/Product Decisions (~25% of questions)

These are REAL spec gaps where Claude genuinely needs your input:
- "Need to decide: permanent discount or just free-during-beta?"
- "$22/month — is that standard rate or grandfathered rate?"
- "Rate limiting — Vercel Edge vs Upstash Redis?"
- "CSRF protection — csrf npm vs custom tokens?"
- "Beta code — hard-code or env var?"
- "Metrics — Resend dashboard or DB table?"
- "Client re-enrollment — restart or let it finish?"

**Recommendation:** These are GOOD questions. They indicate Claude is following the "no assumptions" rule. To reduce them, pre-answer common patterns in your product-marketing-context.md or create a `DECISIONS.md` file for resolved architectural/product decisions.

---

### 4. "Want me to continue?" — Mid-Session Pauses (~10% of questions)

Examples:
- "Ready to move to HIGH-priority fixes, or review first?"
- "Want me to continue with all remaining items?"
- "Want me to knock them all out?"

**Root Cause:** Claude checking in before large batches of work. This is the scope discipline rule in action.

**Recommendation:** When giving tasks, specify scope explicitly: "Fix all 5 items" vs "Fix item 1" — Claude will batch without asking.
